Description:

This is a recipe on how to prepare the famous Yemeni rice and meat dish called mandi.

Mandi may be prepared using beef, mutton, chicken or even camel meat. But today we will use chicken.

Ingredients:

  • Long Grain Basmati Rice
  • Onions
  • Chicken, Bone in Full Chicken Legs
  • Bay leaves
  • Saffron
  • Dry Lime
  • Almonds and if you want, Raisins
  • Additionally, you will need to prepare the Hawaij spice blend consisting of:
    • Coriander seeds
    • Cumin seeds
    • Green Cardamom
    • Cinnamon
    • Cloves
    • Black peppercorns
    • Turmeric

Preparation Instructions:

  1. Toast the hawaij spice blend:
    • Toss all the spices except for the turmeric and bay leaves into a pan and toast for 2-3 mins on medium high until they become fragrant. Don't let them burn.
    • Once they are done toasting, grind them finely together using a coffee grinder and set aside.
  2. Now, take two tablespoons of the spice mix from earlier and set it aside for the rice. The remainder will be used to marinate the chicken.
  3. Mix the spice mix with turmeric and salt and add olive oil to create a paste. Slater this paste over the chicken and leave it to marinate for a few hours.
  4. Next, thinly slice the onions and add them to a pot with oil.
  5. Fry for 15-20 minutes.
  6. Once the onions are golden brown, remove them and set them to drain on paper towels. But leave approximately a third of them in the pot so we can cook them with the rice.
  7. Now, we make the rice broth. Keep frying the onions in the oil, and add the spice blend and dried lime into it. Pierce the dry lime with a knife a few times for added flavor releasage.
  8. Add the bay leaves and bring the water to a boil.
  9. Next, use a steaming rack in the same pot to steam the chicken over the spice water and cover it for 50 minutes.
  10. Once the chicken is really tender, remove it from the pot and pace it on a baking sheet to broil for 10 minutes.
  11. Next, bloom the saffron by grinding them in a mortar and pestle and allowing it to sit for a minute in bowl with 2 teaspoons of hot water.
  12. Now strain the broth that was used to steam the chicken. Reserve the liquid and measure it out 1.5 cups of broth to 1 cup of rice.
  13. Mix the turmeric, broth and bloomed saffron and rice to the pot and simmer covered until the rice is cooked.
  14. Fluff the rice with a fork and leave it to sit undisturbed for 10 minutes.

Serving Instructions:

  • Start by spooning a large bed of the rice onto the plate, garnish with fried onions almonds raisins, and put the chicken legs whole on top.
